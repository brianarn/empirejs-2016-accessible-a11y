Principles & Guidelines

 

POUR

WCAG 2.0

Principles: POUR

 

  • Perceivable
  • Operable
  • Understandable
  • Robust

Perceivable

Can everyone consume your content?

Examples:

  • Text alternatives for non-text content
  • Captions, other alternatives for multimedia content
  • Visible to users of varying levels of sight
  • Functions well with varying ATs

Operable

Can people use your stuff?

Examples:

  • Keyboard navigation (tab, spacebar, enter, etc)
  • Touch interfaces

Understandable

  • Is the content readable? Free of typos?
  • Is the content succinct? Long-form content can be fatiguing to users of ATs.

Robust

Write once, accessible everywhere

The idea here is that it works well in a broad variety of environments. Different browsers, ATs, etc.

Guidelines: WCAG 2.0

POUR codified into rule sets

WCAG Levels

  • A
  • AA
  • AAA

    • Example

    • 3.1.1 (A): Language of page can be programmatically determined
    • 3.1.2 (AA): Language of passages / phrases can be programmatically determined, with some exceptions
    • 3.1.3 (AAA): Mechanism available to identify words or phrases used in an unusual way (jargon etc)

    Another example: Captions on video.

    • A: Having text captions for pre-recorded audio content.
    • AA: Having text captions provided for live audio content.
    • AAA: Sign language interpretation for pre-recorded audio content.

Semantic Markup

Elememts that are not buttons

  • Anchors
  • divs
  • spans
  • Everything that isn't <button>

Put alt attributes on images

alt="" is ok!

ATs will recognize an empty alt to mean that content is meant for presentation, and will skip it

Colors

Oft ignored, but important!

Color Usage

  • Color should not be the only meaningful visual
  • Contrast matters!

This is basically WCAG 2.0 section 1.4

Contrast of 4.5:1 for normal text, 3:1 for large text (18pt and up)

Logos are considered exempt from contrast rules

WAI-ARIA

Often just ARIA

Web Accessibility Initiative for Accessible Rich Internet Applications

Augments HTML in a wide variety of ways, can communicate extra information to ATs

ARIA Roles

Redefine what things are

Can fill in where browsers don't do so well now

Discuss terrible experience of remapping divs and spans via roles

Anecdote about Una and I discussing improvements here

Example: Fixing a button


  <button class="close-button" aria-label="close">

  </button>
